# CONTRIBUTOR GUIDE

## Interactive Preference Collection — SxS Response 

*What to expect, how the workflow runs, and how to complete each task with confidence.*

**Your mission in one line:** Use your own real-world prompts to test our latest models, compare two responses side by side, tell us which you prefer, and keep the conversation going naturally for up to five turns.

| IMPORTANT — Authenticity is everything | The more genuine your prompts, the more valuable your feedback. We are not looking for carefully constructed or artificially difficult questions — we want the things you actually ask AI assistants in real life. |
| :---- | :---- |

## 1  What This Project Is

We are looking for beta testers to try our latest models. Simply use your own real-world prompts — anything you would normally ask ChatGPT, Google, or any other AI assistant — and see how our model responds. For each prompt, you will compare two responses and tell us which one you prefer.  
We are specifically looking for your **everyday AI use cases**: writing help, coding questions, summarizing, brainstorming, explaining concepts, or whatever you actually use AI for. Keep the conversation going naturally for a few back-and-forth exchanges, just like a real session.  
It’s a simple way to influence the future of our models and get paid for your time.

## 2  Task Flow

Each task follows four steps in order:

| Step | Action | Details |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 1 | Enter a prompt | Type or paste a question or request you would naturally send to an AI assistant. |
| 2 | Generate two responses | The platform produces two responses from different model configurations based on your prompt. |
| 3 | Compare side by side | Read both responses in full. Select which one you prefer and optionally note why. |
| 4 | Continue the conversation | Pick a response to continue from and keep chatting naturally for up to five user turns total. |

## 3  Choosing Your Prompt

Your prompt should reflect how you actually use AI in your daily life. **Feel free to reuse prompts or conversation history from your previous interactions with ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Copilot, or other AI assistants** if they reflect your normal day-to-day usage. Please remove any sensitive or confidential information before submitting.

**Good prompts look like:**

| Use Case | Example Prompts |
| :---- | :---- |
| Writing help | “Can you improve the tone of this email?”   “Help me write a cover letter for a product manager role.” |
| Coding questions | “Why is my Python function returning None?”   “How do I make this SQL query faster?” |
| Summarizing | “Summarize this article in a few bullet points.”   “What are the key takeaways from this?” |
| Brainstorming | “Give me ideas for a team offsite activity.”   “What are some ways to save money on groceries?” |
| Explaining concepts | “Explain compound interest simply.”   “What is a neural network?” |

| What to avoid | There is no need to create artificially difficult prompts. Avoid conversations that are intentionally minimal or repetitive just to complete the task. You will be flagged for low effort/repetitive tasks |
| :---- | :---- |

## 4  Continuing the Conversation

After selecting your preferred response, continue the conversation from that point for up to five user turns total. Think of this as a real session — follow up naturally based on what the model said, ask for refinements, or go deeper on part of the answer.  
You do not need to reach five turns. *Stop whenever the conversation feels complete. What matters is that the exchange feels natural and meaningful — not that it hits a specific length.*  
***Please note to have at LEAST 3 turns.*** 

## 5  Tips & Common Pitfalls

| Do ✓ | Avoid ✗ |
| :---- | :---- |
| Use real prompts that reflect how you actually use AI day to day. | Prompts that are intentionally minimal, trivial, or repetitive across tasks. |
| Reuse past conversations from ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Copilot, etc. — scrub sensitive details first. | Artificially difficult or trick prompts designed to stress-test the model rather than reflect real usage. |
| Read both responses fully before picking a preference. | Padding the conversation with empty or nonsensical turns just to reach the five-turn limit. |
| Keep follow-up turns natural — ask questions, request changes, or explore the topic further. | Submitting conversations that include sensitive, confidential, or personally identifiable information. |

## 6  If a Response Fails to Load

Occasionally a response may fail to generate or appear blank. Do not skip the task or refresh the page — use the Resample button instead.

| How to reload a failed response |
| :---- |
| Look for the circular refresh icon (↺) in the top-right corner of the response panel. Hovering over it shows the tooltip "Resample completion." Click it to regenerate that response without losing your task progress.  ![][image1] |

If one or both responses fail to load, resample each one individually using the same (↺) button on each side. If the issue persists after resampling, unclaim the task and reclaim the task again.

## 7  Turn Count Reminder

| Minimum turns required | Maximum turns allowed |
| :---- | :---- |
| 3 user turns | 5 user turns |

Your initial prompt counts as Turn 1\. After selecting a preferred response, you must send **at least 2 follow-up messages** to reach the 3-turn minimum. Keep the conversation natural — follow-up questions, refinements, and deeper dives all count. You may continue up to 5 turns total if the conversation warrants it.

| Reminder | Do not pad turns with empty or filler messages just to hit the minimum. Each turn should reflect something you would genuinely say in a real AI conversation. |
| :---- | :---- |
