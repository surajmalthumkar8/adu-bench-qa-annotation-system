# REVIEWER MODEL — how the person grading your work thinks

Reviewers run a **5-minute, checklist-driven** pass. Your submission is engineered to
survive that exact pass. This file is the reviewer's brain, reverse-engineered from the
Reviewer Guidelines and the quality standards.

## The reviewer's 5-minute script

1. **Minute 1 — Orient**: read question + AI answer, note your verdict, glance at your
   cited pages and quote.
2. **Minutes 2–3 — Verify evidence**: open the cited pages, confirm the quoted text
   appears **word for word** on those pages. Tables/figures → check the PDF Reference tab.
3. **Minute 4 — Confirm verdict**: does the pasted evidence actually support the verdict?
   Correct ⇒ evidence clearly backs the answer. Wrong ⇒ evidence contradicts it, shows it
   incomplete, or shows it unsupported.
4. **Minute 5 — Format check**: quote uses `[Page X] "exact text"` format; pages field
   lists only pages containing the cited evidence; note present if required.

If a task is genuinely too complex to verify in 5 minutes, reviewers are told to
**reject it and move on** — so a submission whose reasoning is hard to follow gets
rejected even when the verdict is right. **Verifiability is a first-class requirement.**

## Approve — ALL must be true

- Verdict is correct given the evidence.
- Evidence quote is verbatim, present on the cited page, in the right format.
- Pages used are accurate and not padded with irrelevant pages.
- A note is present when the situation requires one.

## Reject — ANY is enough

- Verdict wrong.
- Quote missing, paraphrased, or pulled from a different page.
- Pages field empty, wrong, or padded.
- Note clearly required but missing.
- Quote format broken in a way that makes the evidence hard to verify.

## Rejection comments follow a fixed style

One line, names the issue, points to the fix:
- "Verdict should be Wrong. The evidence on page 12 contradicts the answer."
- "Quote does not appear on page 5. Please re-check the cited page."
- "Please add a note. The answer depends on a value from the table on page 8."

(When acting as reviewer, write comments in exactly this shape.)

## The 8 attempter mistakes reviewers are primed to catch

1. Correct because it "sounds plausible" — evidence never checked.
2. Explanation pasted in the Evidence Quotes field instead of a verbatim quote.
3. Page number cited that does not contain the quoted text.
4. Partially-correct answer marked Correct on an exact-text question.
5. Outside knowledge used to justify Correct.
6. PDF Reference skipped when the answer depends on a table/figure/formula.
7. "Not answerable" marked Correct without confirming the evidence is silent.
8. Huge text blocks pasted when one sentence suffices.

## Reviewer tolerance notes

- Minor OCR artifacts / formatting differences in quotes are tolerated; paraphrase is not.
- Multiple pages in the pages field are fine when genuinely needed; padding is not.
- The reviewer asks exactly three final questions: (1) does the verdict follow from the
  evidence? (2) is the quote real, complete, and on the right page? (3) was a note added
  if needed? Engineer every submission so all three are trivially "yes".
